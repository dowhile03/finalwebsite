Why do startups Fail in India?


Author - Kartikey Tyagi

With the Rise of MAKE IN INDIA culture, the number of startups in india has risen exponently. Also, this has been empowered by government by launching different schemes. Plus, initiatives like Stand-Up India, Skill India, the Atal Innovation Mission and Start-Up India have established an ecosystem to encourage entrepreneurship and job creation for the State.

But why these startups fail even after they are supported so much?

1.) Founders’ discord

According to many reports this has been proved that 60% to 70% startups failed just because the founders’ are not aligned on one common idea which is a basic requirement for starting up and have a long journey.



2.) Not hiring the right people

Only Founder does not lead the whole Organisation to the path of success, it is the right people with right mindset engaged in the organization. So, hiring the right people or firing the right people on the right time is need of an organization to follow the path of success. And being able to hire for it is key.





3.) Lack of funding

Sometimes there are great founders with great thought processes who are phenomenal problem solvers. They have good fundamentals in how they build the company, whether it is about people, unit economics, they are able to take multiple aspects into consideration. But they aren’t the best fund raisers.



4.) Lack of perseverance

Starting ups is a tough journey - a journey of contrasts at all times. On the one hand, you want to be smart about aborting a path that isn’t working out, yet on the other hand, when you abort too soon, you are denying yourself the opportunity to solve the problem and thus face challenges.



5.) Knowing when to hustle and when not to

Finding the right strategy for a product or solution is the benchmark of a successful startup. “Sometimes founders can be too conservative or too aggressive - in general there is a thumb rule that if you have figured out the product market fit and the go-to market – then you should push on the pedal. Otherwise throwing money on marketing doesn’t make sense



To summarise It is highly suggested that The approach of entrepreneurs should be to leapfrog and learn from failures rather than to succumb in the name of fortune.
