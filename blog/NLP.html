What exactly is NLP ?

Author - Somya Aggarwal

IT’S ABOUT TO GET REAL!... NATURAL LANGUAGE PROCESSING





The ability of humans to understand other humans by listening the words they speak and recognizing the way they use for speaking is a complex task fulfilled by the brain. Broadening the scope of technology, this ability is now implemented in the technical world.



Computer analyzation and understanding of human language and then acting according to it is referred as Natural Language Processing. It is rightly said- ‘Technology like art is a soaring exercise of the human imagination.’ NLP can be called the next generation technology that emerged out of human beings and some glimpse of this can be seen today in form of English language chat bots- Alexa and Siri.


It is a fascinating technique that can diagnose human health on the basis of patient’s electronic health record and his speech, determine fake news videos and search recruitments and talents based on voice in the present times. The drawback today is the fact that the process of recognizing different languages and manipulating is a challenging task.



Human brain can understand languages with some incorrect pronunciation and grammar but instructing a system to do so is difficult. This is done by some processes that are listed below:



  • • Bags of words- creating group or similar text or sentences
    • Tokenization- segmenting text into words
    • Stop words removal- removing of commonly used pronouns and articles and adding them at last
    • Stemming- slicing the end and beginning of words
    • Lemmatization- limiting words to base form and then processing
    • Topic modelling- clustering text to get base topic

Though the programming languages today provide a huge platform for discovering new things yet there is a lot more to do in this way to make NLP same as or imaginations by performing the above mentioned tasks. The future of NLP is full of challenges and threats but if we move forward with the same speed of advancement we can see Natural Processing Language as a technology in our reach.
